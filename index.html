What I actually do all day is the job this posting describes. How I’d work

Four habits this job actually runs on

Not a strategy. The daily mechanics of making legal technology land on a live matter.

01

Ask before you configure

The request an attorney makes is rarely the problem they have. Sit with the matter, watch the actual steps, then scope the tool to the work — not the other way round.

02

Answer the question, then remove it

Every repeat question is a missing job aid. I answer it once, well — then write the quick reference guide, the checklist, or the annotated screenshot so nobody has to ask again.

03

Make people feel supported, even when the answer is no

Sometimes the tool cannot do it, or should not. Saying so clearly — and offering the next best path — is what keeps attorneys coming back instead of quietly giving up on the platform.

04

Log what you learn, and pass it on

Pain points, enhancement requests, new use cases, what worked. Captured, escalated to the right team or vendor, and folded back into the guides — so the office gets smarter, not just busier.

Why Taft, and why this role
